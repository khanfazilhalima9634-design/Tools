What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed amount paid every month until the home loan is fully repaid.

Each EMI consists of two components:

  • Principal Amount
  • Interest Amount

Principal Amount

The principal is the original amount borrowed from the lender. Every payment toward the principal reduces your outstanding home loan balance.

Interest Amount

Interest is the cost charged by the lender for providing the loan. During the early years of the mortgage, a larger portion of your EMI goes toward interest, while later payments reduce more of the principal.

EMI Formula

EMI = P × R × (1 + R)N ÷ ((1 + R)N − 1)

Where:

  • P = Loan Amount
  • R = Monthly Interest Rate
  • N = Total Number of Monthly Payments

Information Required

  • Home Loan Amount
  • Annual Interest Rate
  • Loan Tenure
  • Payment Frequency

Factors That Affect Your Home Loan EMI

Loan Amount

A higher loan amount generally increases your monthly EMI.

Interest Rate

Lower interest rates reduce your monthly repayment and total borrowing cost.

Loan Tenure

A longer repayment period lowers the monthly EMI but increases the total interest paid.

Down Payment

Making a larger down payment reduces the amount you need to borrow, lowering your monthly EMI and total interest.

Fixed vs Variable Mortgage Rates

Fixed Interest Rate

  • Stable monthly payments
  • Easy budgeting
  • Protection against rising interest rates

Variable Interest Rate

  • Payments may increase or decrease
  • Potential savings if market rates fall
  • Greater repayment uncertainty

Tips to Reduce Your Home Loan EMI
